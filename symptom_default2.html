UNDERSTANDING YOUR SYMPTOMS


Here is a list of symptoms and its better understanding.

1.White Tongue

Whitening of the top layer of the tongue or the presence of white spots or patches on the tongue can be seen with infection, irritation, or chronic inflammation of the surface of the tongue.




2.Stains and Tartar on Teeth

Tartar, also called calculus, forms below and above the gum line. It is rough and porous.



3.Metallic Taste in Mouth.

A metallic taste in your mouth is a type of taste disorder known medically as parageusia. This unpleasant taste can develop suddenly or over longer periods of time.



4.Holes or pits in teeth.

Cavities, also referred to as tooth decay or caries, are holes in the teeth.



5.Brown, black or white Stains on surface of tooth.

The color and appearance of tooth staining (location, extent, surface texture, etc...), as well as the distribution of the teeth affected.



6.Toothache

Toothache refers to pain in and around the teeth and jaws that's usually caused by tooth decay. You may feel toothache in many ways. It can come and go or be constant. Eating or drinking can make the pain worse, particularly if the food or drink is hot or cold. The pain can also be mild or severe.



7.Sensitivity

Tooth sensitivity is causing a short, sharp pain when the area is exposed to hot or cold temperatures through food and beverages â€” or even by the air.



8.Red or Swollen Gums

Swollen gums appear red instead of their normal pink color. Swollen gums, also called gingival swelling, are often irritated, sensitive, or painful. You may also notice that your gums bleed more easily when brushing or flossing your teeth.



9.Bleeding Gums

The gums become inflamed and irritated, which may cause them to bleed when brushing or flossing.



10.Loose teeth

If you have gum disease, then you will likely need a deep cleaning. Since gum disease creates pockets of bacteria and infection between the tooth and gum itself, this can lead to loose teeth.



11.Receeding Gums.

Gingival recession, also known as receding gums, is the exposure in the roots of the teeth caused by a loss of gum tissue and/or retraction of the gingival margin from the crown of the teeth.
