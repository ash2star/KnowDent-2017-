Health Signs One Should Never Ignore

Every disease has some symptom or the other, it's the same in the case of an oral disease. These symptoms may vary from person to person and may have a serious effect on one's health condition.

While taking care of it; it is also necessary to check for newer or more recent symptoms and problems with the teeth or gums. Below are some of the signs which one may have to look out for while doing an oral analysis

  • Bleeding Gums

  • Bad Breath

  • Tooth and gums pain

  • Sores and lumps

  • Jaw Pain

  • Loose teeth

    • When one feels any of the above mentioned discomfort he/she should visit his/her nearest Dentist as soon as possible. To avoid any further complications and to obtain professional help it is advisable to visit a dentist.

    If the pain is just normal or very mild then may also try some home remedies.


    Bleeding Gums

    Gingivitis is the first stage mostly every gum disease. Plaque on one's teeth and at the gumline which wasn't removed by brushing and flossing can infect the gums and lead to the symptoms of gingivitis. If in case of gingivitis, oe's gums may become swollen, tender and sometimes bleed during brushing. This early stage of the disease responds well to good brushing and flossing habits and regular dental checkups.





















    Bad Breath

    Bad breath is also known as Halitosis according to the medical terminology. It is characterized by foul or unpleasant odour of our mouth. It may be depend on one's food intake, or habits such as tabacco consumption. It may also be caused due to poor dental hygiene, oral infections, various dental problems and one's medicine intake.

    Bad breath can be prevented by making use of simple measures such as proper brushing habits(which may include brushing twice daily), quit any baad habits like tabacco and smoking, avoid foods that maybe cause bad order or indigestion. Visit a doctor if the condition is extreme.


    Tooth, Gums and Jaw pain

    Symptoms such cold sores, jaw and mouth pain are signs of stress. Stress may also result in physical and mental disorders. A dentist can help you identify the source of discomfort when related to one's mouth, which is often caused by simple and treatable conditions such as a toothaches or gum disease.

    It's always important to know that pain or discomfort in the jaw can mean alot of things. Knowing these important signs and sympotms, could help save one's life or that of a loved one.


    Loose teeth

    A loose tooth or an unexpected loss of teeth are signs gum disease which may be severe. Tooth loss can also be one of the early signs of osteoporosis, which decreases bone density and weakens your bones.
